An independent contract attorney or freelance lawyer.

Take your pick. Either way, it means your burden is lighter and your client is happy.

My goal is make other attorneys’ lives easier by working for them when they need extra help.

I have almost thirteen years of experience in civil-litigation matters, so I know when to stop researching, and when to start writing. I’ve had my own practice for nearly six years to help small firms and solo practitioners with their litigation matters, and my most requested project to is to engage in motion practice, whether it is writing or responding to a motion.

I use technology to my advantage, so I can easily work with attorneys that I have never met in person. For examples of the types of projects I’ve done, see this post on ways to use an independent contract attorney. I previously worked for seven years as an associate in the Atlanta office of Nelson Mullins Riley & Scarborough LLP.

Niche practice: out-of-state subpoenas

I also have a niche practice in helping out-of-state attorneys have subpoenas issued in Georgia for documents or deposition testimony. I recently presented a CLE on out-of-state subpoenas to a national audience for myLawCLE: Out-of-State Subpoenas: Ending the Game of Hot Potato. I wrote Best Practices for Issuing Subpoenas: Depositions of Georgia Residents in Cases Pending Out of State, published in the Georgia Bar Journal in 2007. In 2011, I wrote a related article, also published in the Georgia Bar Journal, that provides an overview of how to obtain evidence outside the state in state-court litigation: Obtaining Out-of-State Evidence for State Court Civil Litigation: Where to Start? I’ve given a presentation on this topic. I have helped dozens of attorneys obtain enforceable subpoenas in Georgia. I’ve also written several posts on this subject. You can read more about my out-of-state subpoena practice.

Professional activities

In addition to the two Georgia Bar Journal articles, I co-wrote Proactive Strategies to Prevent & Defend Products Liability Cases, published in the A.B.A. Products Liability Newsletter. Before law school
